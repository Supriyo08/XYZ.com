Frequently Asked Questions

1. what is the durability of a PEB Structure?

Durability: Pre-engineered buildings (PEBs) are known for their long lifespan, typically ranging from 25 to 50 years. Their durability is enhanced by the high endurance of steel and the ability to withstand harsh weather conditions.

2. Is PEB construction customisable?

Customization: PEBs offer a high degree of customization, allowing them to be tailored to meet specific industrial standards and individual consumer needs.

3. What is the cost of PEB Construction?

Cost: While the initial investment may vary, PEBs are generally cost-effective over time. They often have lower maintenance and recovery costs compared to conventional buildings.

4. What is the construction time period?

Construction Time: One of the major advantages of PEBs is their faster construction timeline. Large projects like warehouses and airport terminals can often be completed in as little as three to four months.

5. How to maintain a PEB construction?

Maintenance: Regular maintenance of fasteners is essential to ensure the structural integrity of PEBs. Fasteners should be checked frequently for tightness and replaced if damaged, as corrosion can compromise the entire structure.

6. What are the grades of steel used in PEB construction?

Steel Grades: Various grades of steel are used in PEB construction, depending on the specific components. Built-up members, shaped steel, and cold-rolled members each require different steel grades for optimal performance.

7. What are the application of PEB constructions?

Applications: PEBs are versatile and can be used for a wide range of construction projects, including schools, hospitals, office buildings, and more
